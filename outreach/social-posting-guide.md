# Social posting guide

The point of social isn't virality — it's (1) backlinks, (2) steady trickle of
new users, (3) showing Google that people genuinely talk about the site.

**One rule above all others:** never post a link with no context. Always add
value first. The people who ban you aren't mods — they're the community
itself, and you can't recover a bad first impression.

---

## Reddit

### Target subs
- **r/Mahjong** (primary — Riichi-leaning but general mahjong welcome)
- **r/AmericanMahjong** (smaller, but every post is relevant)
- **r/MahjongSoul** (online Riichi — some crossover)
- **r/[YourCity]**, **r/Ask[YourCity]** (local traffic, highest conversion)
- **r/BoardGames** (occasional tangential posts)
- **r/Seniors** (very quiet, but mahjong resonates)

### How to post WITHOUT getting banned
1. **Spend two weeks commenting helpfully before you ever post.** Answer
   beginner questions, link to specific resources — including competitors.
   Build a comment history.
2. **First post: not a link to your site.** Post a useful guide, a question,
   a finding. Establish yourself as someone who contributes.
3. **Third or fourth post: a link to MahjNearMe.** Ideally as a reply to
   someone asking "how do I find a game near me?" — not as a top-level promo.
4. **Disclosure:** if you built it, say so. Reddit respects transparency,
   punishes hidden promotion.

### Good posts vs. bad posts

**Good:**
> "I built a directory of mahjong games in all 50 states — wanted to share it
> with folks here in case it's useful. Entirely free; listings are from publicly
> available info plus organizer submissions. Let me know if I missed your
> group and I'll add it. [link]"

**Bad:**
> "Check out this cool new mahjong site! [link]"

The first is a contribution. The second is an ad.

### Posting cadence
- Once every 10-14 days in r/Mahjong.
- Local subs: only when you have a legitimate reason (new city launch, tourney
  announcement, someone asking).
- Never repost the same thing across multiple subs the same day — this is what
  Reddit's spam filter looks for.

## Facebook

### Groups to join
Search for:
- "[City] Mahjong" — there are hundreds of these
- "American Mah Jongg" — national group, 30k+ members
- "Mah Jongg Lovers" — general
- "Mahjong Mamas" — fast-growing younger-player community
- Regional Jewish community groups (where American Mahjong thrives)

### Rules
1. Read the group description and pinned posts. Most have explicit rules about
   self-promotion.
2. Request permission from admins before posting a link. Many will say yes if
   the resource is genuinely free.
3. If posting is allowed: share the link with a real story. "I couldn't find
   games when I moved to Kansas City so I built a directory." People engage
   with stories, not features.

### Posting cadence
- One post per group per quarter. More than that and admins notice.
- Reply to every comment within 24 hours. Engagement signals > reach.

## Instagram

You're building a brand here, not a lead funnel. IG is slow to pay off.

### What to post
- **Behind-the-scenes of mahjong events** — party photos, hat shots, game
  close-ups. The aesthetic does the work.
- **City guides** — "Mahjong in NYC" carousels with 5-6 top listings per city.
  Drive to /cities/new-york/new-york.
- **Themed posts tied to upcoming holidays** — Derby, Mother's Day, Fourth of
  July, etc. Tease the blog posts.
- **Stories with polls** — "American or Chinese?" "Drop-in or RSVP?" Cheap
  engagement, builds audience.

### Link strategy
- Put MahjNearMe in your bio with a clear CTA ("Find games near you").
- Use a link-in-bio tool (Linktree, Beacons) so you can point to multiple
  posts, blog articles, or city pages at once.
- Stories support stickers with links — use them for every new blog post.

### Posting cadence
- 2-3 posts per week. Less than that, the algorithm stops showing you.
- Stories daily if you can — they're lower effort and drive more profile visits.

## TikTok (the sleeper)

Mahjong + TikTok has been undercovered. The videos that do well:

- **"How to play American Mahjong in 60 seconds"** — blunt, educational,
  highly saved.
- **Themed party ideas** — Derby, Cinco, Mother's Day, New Year.
- **"Mahjong in [city]" micro-guides** — 30-second walkthrough of 2-3 groups.
- **Behind-the-scenes at real parties** — authentic, messy footage >
  produced content.

Even a small follower count (under 2000) can drive real traffic if one video
gets picked up by the algorithm.

## Measuring what works

Set up UTM tags for every social link:
- Instagram bio: `?utm_source=instagram&utm_medium=bio`
- Reddit post: `?utm_source=reddit&utm_medium=post&utm_campaign={{subreddit}}`
- Facebook group: `?utm_source=facebook&utm_medium=group&utm_campaign={{groupname}}`

Check GA4 weekly. Kill the channels that don't convert, double down on the
ones that do. Most founders waste 6 months on the wrong platform because they
never measure.

---

## What NOT to do

- ❌ Post the same link in 10 subreddits the same day.
- ❌ DM strangers with a "check out my site" cold pitch.
- ❌ Buy followers. Instagram's algorithm penalizes fake engagement and
  Google's spam detectors can flag it.
- ❌ Post generic "visit our site!" content with no value.
- ❌ Engage in comment wars, even when you're right.

The goal is to be the obviously-useful resource that everyone else cites. Not
the pushy promoter.
