# Local press pitch — MahjNearMe

## Angle
"Mahjong is having a generational moment — here's where it's happening in [CITY]."
Mahjong's crossover with younger players is a real, ongoing story. Local papers
love trend pieces that have local data. You have the data.

## Who to pitch

**Priority 1 — Local dailies / weeklies (lifestyle desks)**
- Local city paper — lifestyle or "scene" editor
- Regional magazine (e.g., *Chicago Magazine*, *Texas Monthly*, *LA Magazine*)
- Alt-weekly (*The Stranger*, *Dallas Observer*, etc.)

**Priority 2 — Local TV morning shows**
- Morning news lifestyle segments — produce 15-second visuals around themed
  party ideas (Derby, Cinco, Mother's Day). Easy "yes" for them.

**Priority 3 — Niche national outlets**
- *Eater* (if there's a food-and-mahjong angle)
- *The Cut* / *Town & Country* (mahjong's Gen Z crossover)
- *Atlas Obscura* (unusual traditions)

## Finding contacts
Search [name of paper] + "lifestyle editor" or "[City] reporter" + site:twitter.com.
Most editors have their email on their staff bio page.

## What to send if they bite

Have these ready BEFORE you pitch:
1. **A 1-page fact sheet** — 4-5 data points about mahjong growth, a map of
   their city's groups, 2-3 quote-ready organizer names with phone/email.
2. **Two hi-res photos** — one of a game in progress, one of a themed party.
   Get permission first.
3. **A "story bible"** — if they want to write something longer, give them 10
   angles they can pick from. Most reporters will take one verbatim.

## Timing
- **Derby pitch:** send April 22-24 (10 days before the race)
- **Mother's Day pitch:** send April 28-30 (10 days before)
- **Evergreen "mahjong comeback" pitch:** anytime, but avoids major news weeks
- **Holiday / new year:** late December for "New Year's resolution" angle

## Rules
- One pitch per outlet per quarter. More than that and you burn the contact.
- Don't follow up more than once. If they pass, move on.
- If they publish, immediately thank them + share the piece everywhere. This is
  how you become a "good source" worth calling next time.
