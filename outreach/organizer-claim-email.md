# Organizer outreach email — "Your group is on MahjNearMe"

Use this to email every organizer already listed in the directory. Goal: get them
to (a) claim their page, (b) verify their details, (c) share the link with their
players. Each shared link is a backlink + a new cohort of traffic.

Send from a real email address (not noreply@). A human-looking sender gets 3-5x
the reply rate of a system address.

---

## Variables
- `{{organizerName}}` — "Sarah Chen" or "the Brooklyn Mahjong Club"
- `{{groupName}}` — "Brooklyn Mahjong Club"
- `{{cityState}}` — "Brooklyn, NY"
- `{{organizerUrl}}` — `https://www.mahjnearme.com/organizer/{{slug}}`
- `{{senderName}}` — your first name

---

## Subject lines (A/B test these)
1. `{{groupName}} is on MahjNearMe — quick question`
2. `Found your mahjong group — is this info right?`
3. `A small favor for {{groupName}}`

Shortest subject wins most of the time. Keep it under 50 characters.

---

## Email body

> Hi {{organizerName}},
>
> I'm {{senderName}} — I run MahjNearMe, a free directory of mahjong games
> across the US. We added {{groupName}} to our {{cityState}} listings last week
> based on public info, and I wanted to reach out directly to do two things:
>
> **1. Make sure the details are right.** Your page is here:
> {{organizerUrl}}
>
> If anything's off (time, location, skill level, contact info), reply to
> this email and I'll fix it within 24 hours. There's also a "Claim this listing"
> button on the page if you'd like to manage it yourself.
>
> **2. Share it if it's useful.** We get a steady stream of players searching
> for games in {{cityState}}. Your page has your group's contact info front and
> center, so anyone who finds it can get in touch directly. If you'd be willing
> to share the link on your Facebook group, email list, or with a few players
> who might forward it on, it genuinely helps people find you.
>
> Happy to answer any questions. Thanks for keeping the {{cityState}} mahjong
> scene alive.
>
> — {{senderName}}
> MahjNearMe.com

## Follow-up (send 7 days later if no reply)

> Hi {{organizerName}} — just bumping this in case it got buried. No pressure if
> you'd rather not be listed; reply "remove" and I'll take the page down. If the
> details are right, you don't need to do anything.
>
> — {{senderName}}

---

## Tips
- Batch in groups of ~25/day from a real inbox. Faster than that and Gmail
  starts throttling you.
- Personalize the first sentence whenever you can — mention something specific
  from their listing ("I saw your Wednesday sessions at the JCC...").
- Don't attach anything. No images, no PDFs. Plain text gets read.
- If they reply with a correction, fix it IMMEDIATELY and reply with
  confirmation. That turns a lukewarm contact into a referral source.
